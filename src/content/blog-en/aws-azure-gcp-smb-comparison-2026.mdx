Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud Platform. These three names have dominated the cloud market for over a decade, and choosing between them can feel intimidating when you're not an infrastructure expert. The good news: for most SMBs, the best provider isn't the one with the most features, but the one that best aligns with your existing technology ecosystem, compliance requirements, and internal capabilities.

Here's our honest comparison, without unnecessary jargon.

## AWS — The Giant That Invented It All

Amazon is the cloud pioneer. AWS still controls approximately 32% of the global market and offers the most exhaustive service catalog in the industry: over 200 distinct services covering infrastructure, artificial intelligence, databases, security, and much more.

### Advantages for SMBs

**Maturity and reliability**: with 17 years of operation, AWS has an uptime track record that's hard to match. Canadian regions (Montreal and Calgary) offer data residency guarantees that satisfy privacy law requirements.

**Partner ecosystem**: the AWS developer community is the largest in the world. Finding experts, resources, and third-party solutions is straightforward.

**Pricing flexibility**: reserved instances and Savings Plans allow 30 to 72% savings compared to on-demand rates for predictable workloads.

### Disadvantages

AWS's complexity is real. With so many services, navigating the console can be disorienting for teams without dedicated cloud expertise. The learning curve is steeper, and without good governance, costs can spiral quickly — the infamous "bill shock."

**Best for**: SMBs with capable technical teams, tech startups, applications requiring advanced AI/ML services, or organizations migrating from Linux environments.

## Azure — The Natural Choice for Microsoft Environments

Microsoft Azure has become indispensable for organizations already using Microsoft products — which represents the majority of SMBs. If you have Microsoft 365, Teams, or Windows servers, Azure offers native integration that considerably simplifies migration.

### Advantages for SMBs

**Microsoft 365 integration**: Active Directory, Exchange, Teams, SharePoint — everything integrates natively with Azure. Identity and access management is centralized and simplified.

**Hybrid support**: Azure Hybrid Benefit allows you to use your existing Windows Server and SQL Server licenses in the cloud, reducing costs by 40% or more.

**Enterprise compliance**: Azure offers the most compliance certifications in the industry, including specific requirements for financial services and healthcare.

**Strong local support**: Microsoft Canada offers robust enterprise support, a significant advantage for businesses in regulated sectors.

### Disadvantages

Azure can be more expensive for purely Linux workloads. Some services are less mature than AWS equivalents. Billing can be complex to optimize without specialized expertise.

**Best for**: SMBs with Windows/Microsoft 365 environments, regulated sectors (finance, healthcare, government), organizations with .NET applications, or businesses that value strong enterprise support.

## Google Cloud — The Data and AI Specialist

Google Cloud Platform is the youngest of the three major providers, but it excels in specific areas: data analytics, machine learning, and containerized applications. GCP is the product of Google's decades of experience operating infrastructure at planetary scale.

### Advantages for SMBs

**BigQuery and analytics**: if your SMB works with large amounts of data, BigQuery is one of the most powerful and cost-effective analytics tools on the market. Its pay-per-query pricing model makes it accessible for variable workloads.

**Kubernetes**: Google created Kubernetes and GKE (Google Kubernetes Engine) is widely considered the most mature service for container orchestration.

**Competitive pricing**: GCP often offers lower prices than AWS and Azure for comparable workloads, and its automatic Sustained Use Discounts are simple to obtain without any commitment.

**Generative AI**: with Vertex AI and Gemini integration, GCP is particularly well positioned for SMBs looking to build AI capabilities into their products.

### Disadvantages

The partner ecosystem and available talent pool are smaller. Enterprise support can be less responsive. Fewer Canadian regions available (one region in Canada, in Montreal).

**Best for**: data-driven SMBs, startups building AI-powered products, development teams with container expertise, or organizations already in the Google Workspace ecosystem.

## How to Choose Practically

**Start with your existing ecosystem.** If 80% of your infrastructure is Microsoft, Azure is the natural choice. If you have a team familiar with AWS or use many open-source services, AWS is probably right. If your needs center on data analytics or AI product development, GCP deserves serious evaluation.

**Consider your compliance requirements.** Many regulated industries have specific data residency and compliance requirements. AWS, Azure, and GCP all have Canadian regions, but the specific services available in those regions vary — check before committing.

**Don't migrate everything at once.** A phased approach, starting with non-critical workloads, reduces risk and gives your team time to develop the necessary expertise.
